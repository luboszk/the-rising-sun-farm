Kune Kune Pigs

New Zealand's Friendly Grazers

About Kune Kune Pigs

Kune Kune pigs are a small, friendly breed from New Zealand. Their name means "fat and round" in Maori, and they are known for their docile nature and excellent grazing abilities.

These pigs are perfect for small farms and homesteads, as they are easy to handle, good foragers, and produce high-quality pork.

Key Characteristics

  • Small size: 120-240 lbs
  • Short, upturned snout
  • Wattles under the chin
  • Various coat colors
  • Excellent grazers

Benefits

  • Perfect for small farms
  • Excellent temperament
  • Good foragers
  • High-quality pork
  • Low maintenance

Our Kune Kune Herd

At The Rising Sun Farm, we maintain a carefully selected herd of Kune Kune pigs. Our breeding program focuses on maintaining the breed's excellent characteristics while ensuring genetic diversity.

Our pigs are raised on pasture, with access to natural forage and clean water. We practice rotational grazing to maintain healthy pastures and ensure our pigs have access to fresh, nutritious food throughout the growing season.

Products

Pork

Our Kune Kune pork is known for its excellent flavor and tenderness. The meat is well-marbled and perfect for both traditional and modern cooking methods.

Breeding Stock

We occasionally offer breeding stock to other farmers interested in starting or expanding their Kune Kune herd.
